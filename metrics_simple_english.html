Sales metrics
1
Daily Velocity
How many pieces do we sell per day?
Sales
What it means
On average, how many units of this product sell every day. Based on last 30 days only. This is the most important number — all classifications come from this.
Formula
velocity = units sold in last 30 days ÷ 30
Sold 201 meters in 30 days → 201 ÷ 30 = 6.7 meters per day
2
Days of Supply
How many more days will current stock last?
Stock
What it means
If we keep selling at today's speed, stock will run out after this many days. Owner must reorder before this number reaches zero.
Formula
daysOfSupply = stockLeft ÷ velocity If velocity = 0 → show ∞ (not selling = no urgency)
120 meters left, selling 6.7/day → 120 ÷ 6.7 = 18 days of stock left
3
Sold (7d) and Sold (30d)
This week sales vs this month sales
Sales
What it means
Total units sold in last 7 days and last 30 days. Compare these two to see if sales are going up or down. No need for 90 days — too old to be useful for a small shop.
Formula
sold7d = sum of qty where sale date >= today - 7 sold30d = sum of qty where sale date >= today - 30
7d: 59 units (8.4/day)  ·  30d: 201 units (6.7/day) → This week faster than month → selling more now
4
Trend
Is this product selling faster or slower than before?
Sales
What it means
Compare this week's daily speed vs this month's daily speed. If this week is faster — Trending Up. If slower — Slowing Down. If about the same — Steady.
Formula
weekSpeed = sold7d ÷ 7 monthSpeed = sold30d ÷ 30 weekSpeed > monthSpeed × 1.1 → "↑ Trending Up" weekSpeed < monthSpeed × 0.9 → "↓ Slowing Down" else → "→ Steady"
Week: 8.4/day  ·  Month: 6.7/day  ·  8.4 > 7.37 (6.7×1.1) → ↑ Trending Up
5
Revenue (30d)
How much money came in from selling this product this month?
Money
What it means
Total money collected from selling this product in last 30 days. This is not profit — it is the full selling amount before costs.
Formula
revenue = sum of (qty × selling_price) for each sale in last 30 days
201 meters × ₹120 per meter → ₹24,120 revenue this month
Stock metrics
6
Stock Left
How much is sitting in the shop right now?
Stock
What it means
Total quantity remaining across all batches of this product. Every time a bill is made, this number goes down.
Formula
stockLeft = sum of batch.quantity for all batches of this product
Batch 1: 80 mtr  ·  Batch 2: 40 mtr → 120 meters total stock left
7
Stock Remaining %
Out of what we bought, how much is still unsold?
Stock
What it means
If we bought 200 meters and 120 is still here — 60% is unsold. High % + old batch = problem. Low % = good, almost sold out.
Formula
remainingPct = stockLeft ÷ original_quantity × 100 ⚠ Must store original_quantity at purchase time
Bought 200 mtr, 120 still here → 60% remaining — 40% sold so far
8
Oldest Batch Age
How old is the oldest unsold stock?
Stock
What it means
Number of days since the oldest batch was purchased. 30+ days with a lot still unsold = money sitting idle. Used together with remaining % to detect old stock.
Formula
maxBatchAge = max of (today - created_at) in days for batches where qty > 0
Batch A: 5 days old  ·  Batch B: 12 days old → Oldest batch = 12 days
Money metrics
9
Margin %
How much profit do we make on each unit?
Money
What it means
Bought at ₹85, sell at ₹120 — what % profit is that? Higher is better. Below 15% is a warning. Use per-unit prices, not batch totals.
Formula
margin = (sellPrice - buyPrice) ÷ buyPrice × 100
Buy ₹85, Sell ₹120 → (120 - 85) ÷ 85 × 100 = 41% margin ✓ Healthy
10
Catalog Average Velocity
What is the normal selling speed for our shop?
Used for classification
What it means
Average selling speed of all products in the shop. This is the baseline. A product is "fast" or "slow" only compared to this number — not against any fixed number.
Formula
avgVelocity = sum of velocity of all products (only products with at least 1 sale) ÷ count of those products
5 products: 6.7 + 3.2 + 1.1 + 4.5 + 0.8 = 16.3 → avgVelocity = 16.3 ÷ 5 = 3.26/day
One rule to remember: Never use fixed numbers like "sold more than 10" or "older than 5 days". Always compare against avgVelocity — the shop's own average. A small shop and a big shop will have different averages, and the system adjusts automatically.
Label When to show it What owner should do
🔥 High Selling Selling fast Fast Moving velocity >= avgVelocity × 1.5
This product sells 1.5 times faster than the shop average. Example: avg is 3.2/day, this product is 6.7/day — 6.7 is more than 4.8 (3.2×1.5) so it is High Selling.
 Buy more stock. Do not let it run out. Check if reorder point alert is set.
😐 Normal Selling at average pace Steady velocity between avgVelocity × 0.5 and avgVelocity × 1.5
Between half and 1.5 times the shop average. No problem. Most products should be here.
 No action needed. Keep watching.
📉 Low Selling Selling slowly Slow Moving velocity > 0 AND velocity < avgVelocity × 0.5
Some sales happening but very slowly — less than half the shop average. Money is moving slowly through this product.
 Try a small discount. Do not buy more stock. Check if display position helps.
💀 Dead Stock Not selling at all Non-Moving velocity = 0 AND stockLeft > 0 AND last sale > 30 days ago
Zero sales for 30+ days but stock is still sitting. Money is locked and not moving at all.
 Reduce price immediately. Bundle with fast product. Talk to vendor about return.
📦 Old Stock Old batch, mostly unsold Aged Inventory maxBatchAge >= 30 days AND remainingPct >= 50% AND velocity < avgVelocity × 0.3
Batch is 30+ days old AND more than half is still unsold AND selling very slowly. All three must be true together.
 Give festival offer or bulk discount. Do not reorder this product yet.
🆕 New Product Just added, no sales yet New velocity = 0 AND maxBatchAge < 30 days
No sales yet but batch is less than 30 days old. This is not dead stock — it is just new. Give it time.
 Wait 30 days. Put it in a visible spot. Tell customers about it.
Important: Old Stock needs original_quantity stored in each batch. Without it you cannot calculate remaining %. Add this one field when saving a purchase: original_quantity: qty alongside the normal quantity: qty.

Bug 1 — High Selling always picks top 3 products
Problem
If your shop has only 4 products, 3 of them will always show as "High Selling" even if none of them sold well. Top 3 is just a rank — not a real classification.
Fix
// Remove this: sortedProducts.slice(0, 3) // Use this instead: analytics.filter(a => a.velocity >= avgVelocity * 1.5 )
✗
Bug 2 — Low Selling checks units sold, not speed
Problem
Current check: qty sold ≤ 10. But 9 units in 2 days is fast. 9 units in 30 days is slow. The number alone means nothing without knowing the time.
Fix
// Remove this: p.qty <= 10 // Use this instead: a.velocity > 0 && a.velocity < avgVelocity * 0.5
✗
Bug 3 — Old Stock flags batches after just 5 days
Problem
Bought on Monday — by Saturday it shows as "Old Stock". That is not old stock. 5 days is normal business. Need at least 30 days + mostly unsold + selling very slowly.
Fix
// Remove this: ageDays >= 5 // Use this instead: ageDays >= 30 && remainingPct >= 50 && velocity < avgVelocity * 0.3
✗
Bug 4 — Velocity uses all-time sales, not last 30 days
Problem
Current code takes total sales of all time and divides by 30. If a product sold 200 units 6 months ago but nothing this month — it still shows a high velocity. That is wrong.
Fix
// Remove this: const soldUnits = salesData.qty; const velocity = soldUnits / 30; // Use this instead: const cutoff = Date.now() - 30 * 86400000; const soldUnits = sale_items .filter(i => i.product_id === p.id && new Date(i.created_at) >= cutoff ) .reduce((s,i) => s + i.qty, 0); const velocity = soldUnits / 30;
✓
Add original_quantity when saving a purchase batch
Why
Right now you only store current quantity (goes down as you sell). You need the original amount bought to calculate how much % is still unsold. One extra field fixes this.
Add in saveStockEntry()
batches.push({ id: batchId, product_id: productId, quantity: qty, original_quantity: qty, // ← ADD purchase_price: price, created_at: new Date() });
